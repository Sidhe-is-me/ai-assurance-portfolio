# AI Assurance Portfolio

Sample work and talks on **AI governance and testing**, with a focus on **inclusion** — what I call **Ally HCI**.

I work on one question: *how do you show an AI system can be trusted by the people who actually depend on it?* My background is in accessibility and human-factors work, where I use a simple loop — take a rule, turn it into a check, gather evidence, record the result, and fix what fails. This repo applies that same loop to AI, with the inclusion and human-factors lens that pure-governance reviews often miss.

## What's here

- **[Trustworthy-AI Conformance Assessment](trustworthy-ai-conformance-assessment.md)** — a full work sample that checks a sample public-sector AI system against **NIST AI RMF 1.0**, the **EU AI Act** (high-risk / human-oversight), and **Section 508 / WCAG 2.1**. It includes a table that traces each requirement to a control, a test, the evidence, and a finding; the most important findings; a look at human oversight; and fixes followed through to closure. Built on a made-up, non-client system.

- **[Talks & Speaking](talks.md)** — *AI + Accessibility: Holding AI Systems to Inclusive Standards*, on testing AI for accessibility and trustworthiness at the same time.
