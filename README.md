# AI Assurance Portfolio

Work samples and talks at the intersection of **AI governance / test & evaluation**, **accessibility (Section 508 / WCAG 2.1)**, and **human factors**.

I spend my time on a single question: *how do you prove an AI system is trustworthy for the people who actually depend on it?* My background is 14+ years running the **rule → control → evidence → finding → remediation** loop on federal accessibility programs. This repo shows that same discipline applied to AI assurance — with the accessibility and human-factors lens that pure-governance reviews tend to miss.

## Contents

- **[Trustworthy-AI Conformance Assessment](trustworthy-ai-conformance-assessment.md)** — a full work sample assessing a representative public-sector AI system against **NIST AI RMF 1.0**, **EU AI Act** (high-risk / human-oversight), and **Section 508 / WCAG 2.1**. Includes a controls-traceability matrix (requirement → control → test method → evidence → finding), prioritized findings, a human-oversight assessment, and remediation-to-closure recommendations. Built on a non-client, illustrative system.

- **[Talks & Speaking](talks.md)** — *AI + Accessibility: Holding AI Systems to Inclusive Standards*, on evaluating AI for accessibility and trustworthiness together.
